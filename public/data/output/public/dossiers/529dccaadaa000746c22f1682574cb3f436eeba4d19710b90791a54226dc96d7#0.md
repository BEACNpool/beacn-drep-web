## Failure Mode Analysis
_grounded: yes_

Illusory TVL growth. The headline objective is durable liquidity, and the two classic ways to appear to achieve it without achieving it are ADA price appreciation inflating dollar-denominated TVL, and TVL that arrives for reasons unrelated to the programme. The proposal names both and excludes them: 'ADA price effects and non-PRIME attributable TVL excluded under the attribution methodology.' That the failure modes are named is meaningful; that the attribution methodology enforcing the exclusion is not in the supplied record means the defence cannot be verified here.

Mercenary liquidity. Incentive-driven TVL that departs when incentives stop is the standard failure mode of a programme of this shape. The proposal's language ('durable liquidity', 'retained economic activity', 'capital retention') indicates awareness of it, but the supplied record contains no retention metric, no post-programme persistence target, and no lock-up or vesting mechanism that would bind incentivized capital. This is the largest unmitigated-on-the-record failure mode, and it is precisely the one the missing budget breakdown and attribution methodology would speak to.

Deployment without a gap analysis. Phase 3 deploys incentives and capital, but the analysis that determines where they should go is itself Phase 2 output. The mitigation is explicit — Phase 3 releases only 'after the Operating Group affirms the Phase 3 release plan', behind a Month 4 gate — so the failure mode of deploying capital into a gap analysis that never materializes is structurally gated rather than merely promised.

Custody and administration failure. Funds sit in a smart-contract-administered reserve account before disbursement. The framework is audited (TxPipe, MLabs) per the anchor, but those reports were not supplied, and the custody arrangement is left partly open ('custody solutions as required'). A contract or custody failure would expose an amount up to the undisbursed balance of ₳120,000,000.

Governance-weight side effect. A large treasury balance held before disbursement could otherwise carry voting weight. The proposal removes this: 'abstain delegation while funds are held before disbursement.'

Recovery path. Six return triggers exist 'for unused, unearned, unreleased, or excess funds', which is the correct remedy shape for every failure mode above that ends in undeployed or unearned capital. Their exact text, thresholds, and enforcement mechanism are not in the record, so the remedy is present in principle and unverified in detail.

Capacity failure at enactment. If the applicable NCL under TREASURY-01a has insufficient remaining capacity, the proposal states the withdrawal is conditional and should not proceed in full — a self-executing failure mode with a defined outcome, though whether it self-executes on-chain or by administrative discretion is not stated.
