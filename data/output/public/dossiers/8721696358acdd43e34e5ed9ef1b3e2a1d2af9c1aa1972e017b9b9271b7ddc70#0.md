## Risk Analysis
_grounded: yes_

The proposal discloses its risk surface directly, which is to its credit: ADA-appreciation risk (principal is sold for USDM and 'may underperform holding ADA if ADA rises significantly'), USDM depeg / custody / liquidity / redemption risk, smart contract risk, yield underperformance, and multisig operational risk. Mitigations are named: independent multisig custody, drawdown-triggered review, wind-down on missed reporting, monthly public reports, third-party assurance at deployment / month 6 / month 12, and Section 7-compliant holding accounts delegated to abstain.

The controls do not fully cohere. The Risk Summary sets governance review at >8% drawdown and 'mandatory review at 10% drawdown'; the Early Termination table sets the mandatory-review trigger at >20%. These cannot both be the operative threshold, and the document does not say which governs. Nor does it say whether drawdown is measured in ADA or USD — a decisive ambiguity, because the principal is converted to USDM at deployment, so an ADA rally produces large ADA-denominated drawdown with zero USD loss, while a USDM depeg or trading loss produces the reverse. At the 20% figure, up to 1,800,000 ADA — twice the entire modeled 12-month yield of 900,000 ADA — could be lost before mandatory review is compelled.

The structural risk the document does not engage is enforceability. A TreasuryWithdrawals action transfers ADA unconditionally on-chain. Every protective term here — return at month 12, midpoint yield distribution, no-unilateral-custody, wind-down control — is a governance and multisig arrangement, not an escrow, a smart-contract lock, or a legal instrument. The record describes no mechanism that compels return if the council does not perform. This is compounded by the custody specifics being absent: no multisig threshold (no m-of-n anywhere in the document), no signer keys, no holding address (the anchor says accounts 'will be published prior to on-chain submission'), no signer-replacement procedure, and no conflict-of-interest policy for council members who are principals of other commercial Cardano projects (Snek, Surf, Monetra). The council naming is itself inconsistent — 'Moneta' in the abstract, 'Monetra' twice in the rationale.

Counterparty risk concentrates on USDM, and the record is silent on it: no issuer named, no reserve attestation, no redemption mechanics, no depth figure, no depeg history. The entire 9,000,000 ADA principal passes through this asset.

Against doctrine: BEACN's values hierarchy places treasury stewardship and downside protection above ecosystem growth, and directs that where 'treasury upside is plausible but non-verifiable', the record be judged with a caution penalty. That is the exact shape of this action — the upside is modeled and internally coherent, and the evidence for it is entirely proposer-supplied.

Other providers or mechanisms. The record names none. No comparison to market rates for liquidity provision appears anywhere.
