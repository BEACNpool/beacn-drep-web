The rulebook

What earns each verdict

A verdict is never a vibe. First a few hard gates can force a conservative outcome, then a public, weighted score routes the action. Here's what pushes an action toward each result.

Conservative gates come first. Before any scoring, BEACN automatically abstains on stale data, missing baseline evidence, or an unknown action type — and returns needs more info when a treasury ask arrives without a completed research dossier. Speed never overrides safety.
✓ Yesapprove

Well-evidenced and scores clearly positive.

  • Verifiable, pinned anchor / context document.
  • Risk flags low or mitigated.
  • For treasury: complete dossier, spending within sustainable fee inflow and the rolling-window budget, clear milestones.
  • Net score clears the positive threshold.
✕ Noreject

Identifiable harm, excess cost, or broken treasury discipline.

  • High unmitigated risk or strong negative flags.
  • For treasury: unsustainable outflow, severe recipient concentration, or no milestones.
  • A forced No when the rolling-window budget is already depleted.
  • Net score crosses the negative threshold.
— Abstainno directional vote, on purpose

Can't responsibly take a side — discipline, not indecision.

  • Evidence too thin or stale, or context/anchor unpinned.
  • A hard blocker or high unmitigated risk is present.
  • Score too close to neutral to justify a direction.
? Needs more infoa request, not a refusal

A high-impact ask that lacks the evidence to decide.

  • Treasury action missing its deep-research dossier: budget, feasibility, risks, alternatives, failure modes.
  • Always lists exactly what's missing — and what would change the vote.

When values conflict, BEACN applies a fixed order: constitutional integrity & protocol safety → treasury stewardship → evidence quality → public benefit → speed. The higher value always wins.

Verify the rules — weights are public and versioned (scoring_weights.json), thresholds live in source (engine), and every action type has its own doctrine (treasury, parameters, hard forks, values hierarchy).

Why BEACN

A delegate that can't be lobbied, bought, or tired out

The same inputs always produce the same vote, every decision shows its work and can be independently replayed, and there is no channel for money, hype, or fatigue to change the outcome. BEACN treats your stake's voice like a fiduciary — conservative when evidence is thin, honest about what it doesn't know, and disciplined with the treasury.

🛡️ Incorruptible by design

No private lobbying, no backroom overrides, no delegation-size politics. The rules are public and identical for everyone.

🔎 Public evidence only

Decisions use only declared, citable, independently fetchable sources — never private chats, hidden prompts, or undeclared data.

♻️ Reproducible receipts

Every vote cites the exact doctrine version and input hashes, so anyone can re-run it and reach the same result.

⚖️ Conservative fiduciary

When evidence is weak it abstains rather than guesses. "Not enough evidence yet" is discipline, not failure.

🏛️ Constitution first

Protocol safety and constitutional integrity rank above everything — ahead of speed or social momentum.

💰 Treasury stewardship

Treats the Net Change Limit as a ceiling, not a target, favoring sustainable spend. Pro-accountability, not anti-spending.

This is a move from "trust me" governance — personalities, politics, and persuasion — to "verify me" governance: rules-based, evidence-based, and accountable by construction rather than by promise. This is what the future of decisions looks like.

Who it's for

  • ADA holders who value process integrity over personality.
  • Delegators who can't track every governance action but want a principled, predictable proxy.
  • Anyone who wants protocol safety and treasury sustainability prioritized over hype.
  • Small holders especially — a rule that can't be lobbied or bought gives every delegator an equal voice.
