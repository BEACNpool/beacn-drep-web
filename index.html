How these votes are made — with zero human interference

No person picks how BEACN DRep votes. A program does the whole job and shows its work. Every input, rule, and result is public and reproducible, so anyone can check that the vote on chain matches the rules.

1 · Read

It reads every live governance action directly from the Cardano chain.

2 · Decide

A deterministic, open-source engine scores each action against published doctrine — the same inputs always produce the same verdict.

3 · Vote & prove

It signs and submits the vote on chain and publishes the rationale, with hashes you can re-check. No human in the loop.

Audit it yourself — everything is open source: engine (core) · doctrine (soul) · data (resources) · this site (web)

Why delegate to BEACN

BEACN DRep is governance you can verify instead of governance you have to trust. The same inputs always produce the same vote, every decision shows its work and can be independently replayed, and there is no channel for lobbying, money, hype, or fatigue to change the outcome. It treats your stake's voice like a fiduciary — conservative when the evidence is thin, honest about what it doesn't know, and disciplined with the treasury.

🛡️ Incorruptible by design

No private lobbying, no backroom overrides, no delegation-size politics. The rules are public and identical for everyone.

🔎 Public evidence only

Decisions use only declared, citable, independently fetchable sources — never private chats, hidden prompts, or undeclared data.

♻️ Reproducible receipts

Every vote cites the exact doctrine version and input hashes, so anyone can re-run it and reach the same result.

⚖️ Conservative fiduciary

When the evidence is weak it abstains rather than guesses. Saying "not enough evidence yet" is discipline, not failure.

🏛️ Constitution first

Protocol safety and constitutional integrity rank above everything else — ahead of speed or social momentum.

💰 Treasury stewardship

Treats the Net Change Limit as a ceiling, not a target, and favors spending near sustainable fee inflows. Pro-accountability, not anti-spending.

This is a move from "trust me" governance — personalities, politics, and persuasion — to "verify me" governance: rules-based, evidence-based, and accountable by construction rather than by promise.

Who it's for

  • ADA holders who value process integrity over personality.
  • Delegators who can't track every governance action but want a principled, predictable proxy.
  • Anyone who wants protocol safety and treasury sustainability prioritized over hype.
  • Small holders especially — a rule that can't be lobbied or bought gives every delegator an equal voice.

How BEACN decides — what earns each verdict

A verdict is never a vibe. It is the output of a public, versioned rule set: first a few hard gates can force a conservative outcome, then a weighted score routes the action. Here is what pushes an action toward each result.

Conservative gates come first. Before any scoring, BEACN automatically abstains when the data is stale, baseline evidence is missing, or the action type has no rubric — and returns needs more info when a treasury ask arrives without a completed research dossier. Speed never overrides safety.
✓ Yesapprove

Well-evidenced and scores clearly positive.

  • Has a verifiable, pinned anchor / context document.
  • Risk flags are low or carry mitigation evidence.
  • For treasury: a complete research dossier, spending within sustainable fee inflow and the rolling-window budget, with clear milestones.
  • Net score clears the positive threshold.
✕ Noreject

Carries identifiable harm, excess cost, or breaks treasury discipline.

  • High unmitigated risk, or strong negative flags.
  • For treasury: unsustainable outflow, severe recipient concentration, or no milestones.
  • A forced No when the rolling-window treasury capacity is already depleted.
  • Net score crosses the negative threshold.
— Abstainno directional vote, on purpose

BEACN can't responsibly take a side — discipline, not indecision.

  • Evidence is too thin or stale, or the context/anchor is unpinned.
  • A hard blocker or high unmitigated risk is present.
  • The score is too close to neutral to justify a direction.
? Needs more infoa request, not a refusal

A high-impact ask that lacks the evidence needed to decide.

  • Treasury action missing its deep-research dossier: budget, feasibility, risks, alternatives, failure modes.
  • Always lists exactly what is missing — and what would change the vote.

When values conflict, BEACN applies a fixed order: constitutional integrity & protocol safety → treasury stewardship → evidence quality → public benefit → speed. The higher value always wins.

Verify the rules yourself — the weights are public and versioned (scoring_weights.json), the thresholds live in auditable source (engine), and every action type has its own doctrine (treasury, parameters, hard forks, values hierarchy).
