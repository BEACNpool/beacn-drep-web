# NO → YES Checklist (Governance Proposals)

Use this before resubmitting or refining a proposal that previously received a NO recommendation.

## 1) Evidence integrity (required)
- [ ] Anchor document is reachable and pinned/reproducible.
- [ ] Source links are stable (avoid brittle branch-path links when possible).
- [ ] Core claims map directly to receipts.

## 2) TreasuryWithdrawals-specific (high priority)
- [ ] Budget is line-itemized (not broad buckets).
- [ ] Disbursement is milestone-gated (not lump-sum by default).
- [ ] Milestones have objective completion criteria.
- [ ] Downside risks + mitigation plan are explicit.
- [ ] Sustainability path is explicit (especially if recurring funding is implied).
- [ ] Proposal explains why now, given treasury pressure and opportunity cost.

## 3) ParameterChange / HardFork-specific
- [ ] SPO/operator impact envelope is documented (CPU/RAM/network/storage).
- [ ] Rollback or containment plan is documented.
- [ ] Rollout criteria and halt conditions are explicit.
- [ ] Side effects and monitoring signals are listed.

## 4) InfoAction-specific
- [ ] Governance intent is concrete (what decision this informs).
- [ ] Claims are evidence-backed, not narrative-only.
- [ ] Expected measurable outcome is defined.

## 5) Decision quality boosters (cross-cutting)
- [ ] Alternatives considered and rejected are documented.
- [ ] Unknowns are acknowledged (with plan to close them).
- [ ] Tradeoffs are explicit (benefit vs cost vs risk).
- [ ] Constructive revisions from prior NO rationale are addressed point-by-point.

## Notes
- A NO is not final destiny; it usually means the burden of proof and risk controls were not yet sufficient.
- Strong proposals reduce ambiguity, reduce execution risk, and improve auditability.
